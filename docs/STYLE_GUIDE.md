# Alqudus Express Trucking LLC — Style Guide

## 1. Brand direction

Alqudus Express Trucking LLC should feel professional, reliable, direct, and driver-focused. The brand should speak to owner-operators who already have their own trucks and are looking for steady contract/load opportunities.

The visual style should be clean and bold, with a trucking/logistics look rather than a generic corporate website. The website should feel trustworthy enough for drivers to submit their information and professional enough for customers, brokers, and partners to take the company seriously.

## 2. Brand personality

Use a tone that is:

- Clear and practical
- Professional but not too formal
- Confident and direct
- Driver-friendly
- Honest about the business model

Avoid language that makes it sound like the company provides trucks to drivers. The message should be clear: drivers must have their own truck, and Alqudus Express helps connect them with contract/job opportunities.

## 3. Core message

Primary message:

**Bring your truck. We help bring the work.**

Alternative headings:

- Owner-operators wanted
- Drive under your own truck, with support from Alqudus Express
- Contract opportunities for qualified owner-operators
- Professional trucking opportunities based from Saint Cloud, MN

## 4. Logo usage

Use the approved circular Alqudus Express Trucking LLC logo as the main brand mark.

The circular logo is the primary brand emblem for the website and should appear in major brand moments such as the hero, footer, and other brand sections.

The navbar logo is temporary text until a horizontal logo is created. Use `ALQUDUS EXPRESS` in the navigation bar for now.

Recommended usage:

- Website header: small logo icon with text beside it, or full logo if space allows
- Hero section: full logo can appear larger as a brand element
- Footer: smaller logo with company contact details
- Driver application page: smaller logo at the top for trust and consistency

Avoid:

- Stretching or distorting the logo
- Placing the logo on busy backgrounds
- Using low contrast backgrounds
- Recoloring the logo outside the approved palette
- Making the circular logo too large in small UI areas

## 5. Color palette

The color palette is based on the approved logo.

Primary colors should be inspired by the logo: navy, royal blue, white, and light gray. Keep the palette professional and restrained.

### Primary colors

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---:|---|
| Deep Navy | `#001D44` | Main headings, footer, strong buttons, text accents |
| Express Blue | `#004CB2` | Primary buttons, links, highlights, active states |
| Midnight Navy | `#00153B` | Dark sections, outlines, hover states |

### Supporting colors

| Name | Hex | Usage |
|---|---:|---|
| Steel Blue | `#3D5881` | Secondary accents, muted icons, borders |
| Cool Slate | `#7E8EA6` | Secondary text, dividers, metadata |
| Light Steel | `#C9CFDA` | Card borders, pale backgrounds, separators |
| White | `#FFFFFF` | Main background and contrast |
| Off White | `#F7F9FC` | Page background, form sections, dashboard cards |

## 6. Typography

The logo uses a bold italic trucking/speed style. The website should support that look without trying to copy it everywhere.

Recommended web fonts:

### Headings

Use a strong geometric font such as:

- **Montserrat**
- **Poppins**
- **Inter Tight**
- **Barlow Condensed** for trucking-style large headings

### Body text

Use a clean readable font such as:

- **Inter**
- **Poppins**
- **Roboto**

Suggested pairing:

- Headings: `Montserrat` or `Barlow Condensed`
- Body: `Inter`

## 7. UI style

The website should use:

- Clean white and off-white backgrounds
- Navy text and blue highlights
- Rounded but not overly soft cards
- Strong call-to-action buttons
- Clear form fields
- Mobile-first spacing
- High contrast for readability

The design should feel modern, but not too decorative. The company is a trucking/dispatch business, so clarity and trust matter more than fancy animations.

The design should feel like a professional American trucking and logistics website, with clear driver-focused messaging and a strong owner-operator recruitment flow.

Mobile UX should feel like a native app with large touch targets, an app-style header, bottom navigation where useful, and card-based sections. Avoid making mobile feel like a squeezed desktop layout.

## 8. Buttons

### Primary button

Use for important actions like **Apply Now**.

- Background: `#004CB2`
- Text: white
- Border radius: 8px to 12px
- Font weight: 700
- Hover: darker navy `#001D44`

Example labels:

- Apply as Owner-Operator
- Start Application
- Contact Dispatch
- Join Our Driver Network

### Secondary button

Use for less urgent actions.

- White background
- Navy text
- Navy or light steel border
- Hover: off-white background

Example labels:

- Learn More
- View Requirements
- Call Us

## 9. Layout guidance

### Homepage structure

Recommended sections:

1. Header/navigation
2. Hero section with owner-operator message
3. How it works
4. Driver requirements
5. Why work with Alqudus Express
6. Application call-to-action
7. Contact/footer

### Application page structure

Recommended sections:

1. Short intro explaining who should apply
2. Requirements checklist
3. Driver application form
4. Document upload section, if included
5. Confirmation message after submission

## 10. Imagery direction

Use images that show:

- Semi-trucks on highways
- Owner-operators / professional drivers
- Clean truck yards or road scenes
- Minnesota / northern road atmosphere where appropriate

Avoid:

- Overly generic stock business images
- Images suggesting the company owns a large fleet, unless true
- Images showing company-provided trucks for drivers
- Busy backgrounds behind important text

## 11. Icon style

Use simple line icons or solid icons in navy/blue.

Recommended icon themes:

- Truck
- Route
- Clipboard/application
- Shield/compliance
- Document
- Dollar/commission
- Phone/message
- Location pin

## 12. Voice and wording rules

Use wording that is honest and clear.

Good wording:

- “For owner-operators with their own truck”
- “Apply to join our driver network”
- “We connect qualified drivers with contract opportunities”
- “Drivers are responsible for their own equipment”

Avoid wording:

- “We provide trucks”
- “Get a truck from us”
- “Guaranteed loads” unless the client confirms it
- “Employment” if drivers are independent contractors/owner-operators

## 13. Form design

Forms should be simple and mobile-friendly.

Recommended fields for first prototype:

- Full name
- Phone number
- Email
- City/state
- Do you own your truck?
- Truck type
- Trailer type
- Years of experience
- CDL status
- Insurance status
- Preferred routes/lanes
- Availability
- Message/notes

For later phases:

- CDL upload
- Insurance upload
- W-9 upload
- Truck registration upload
- Medical card upload
- Expiry dates

## 14. Dashboard style, future phase

If an admin dashboard is built later, it should follow the same palette and layout.

Dashboard cards can include:

- New applications
- Active drivers
- Pending documents
- Expiring documents
- Open jobs
- Completed jobs
- Commission earned
- Payments pending

Keep dashboard design simple, with clear tables and status labels.

## 15. Status colors

Use these carefully and consistently:

| Status | Suggested color |
|---|---:|
| Approved / Active | `#15803D` |
| Pending | `#B45309` |
| Expiring soon | `#F59E0B` |
| Expired / Rejected | `#B91C1C` |
| Info / New | `#004CB2` |

## 16. Accessibility notes

- Keep text high contrast
- Do not place blue text on dark navy unless it remains readable
- Use at least 16px body text on mobile
- Buttons should be large enough to tap easily
- Form labels should always be visible
- Do not rely on color alone for status; include text labels

## 17. Recommended website feel

The finished site should feel like:

**A clean driver recruitment and operations platform for a Minnesota-based owner-operator trucking network.**

It should not feel like a flashy freight marketplace or a generic trucking template. The focus is trust, simple application, and clear business purpose.
